## 0. Why this slice (the decision trail)

The comprehensiveness assessment (2026-06-28) found the spine richly covers the build *middle* (Plan → Build → Review) but the loop's **front — Discover —** is covered today only by a GATE + TEMPLATE (`conformance/discovery-complete.sh`, `templates/FEATURE-REQUEST-TEMPLATE.md`), never a craft SKILL. `continuous-discovery` is to that gate what `tdd` is to the test runner and `evals` is to the eval gate: the *how* that produces what the gate checks. It is the front of the loop, the problem-space partner to `design`'s solution-space.

### The reframe (owner-ratified): the human↔AI discovery **PARTNER** craft — not "agent does discovery"
The human is the PO. The agent does **not** decide what to build. The skill's craft is to make Teresa Torres' continuous discovery *accessible and honest* for the human: it structures the opportunity space, surfaces the **riskiest assumption** the plan is betting on, holds the work to **outcome over output**, and designs the **smallest test/bet** that would prove or kill an assumption before a slice is built. This partner framing is the kit-distinctive angle and the source of the `discovery partner` marker — a generic "run discovery for me" tutorial fails it.

### It is a KIT-ORIGINAL, not a superpowers replacement
Bricks #1–8 each replaced a superpowers skill (content + discovery). **Superpowers has no continuous/product-discovery skill** — its `brainstorming` is solution-space (= the kit's `design`). So #10, like `evals`, *adds* a craft superpowers lacks: problem-space product discovery. The `skill-spine` claim wording must reflect this — do NOT say "replaces superpowers" for `continuous-discovery`.

### Naming (owner-ratified): `continuous-discovery`, NOT bare `discovery`
"Discovery" is already spent in this kit on a *different* meaning — **skill**-discovery: the keystone is "the discovery keystone," the Orchestrator's first hat is "Discovery (start here)," the discipline is "the discovery discipline." First principle **one term, one meaning**: a second bare `discovery` would manufacture a collision inside the kit's core vocabulary. The keystone's entrenched meaning stays; the new craft takes the precise, industry-standard name. `continuous-discovery` (Torres) over `product-discovery` (Cagan) because **"continuous" is load-bearing** — the opposite of a one-time discovery *phase* — and "continuous discovery in an agentic world" is the kit's stated identity hook. The hyphen is a non-issue (`verification`, `debugging`, `worktrees` already broke "one short word"; clarity is the constraint, not length).

### Intent (unchanged): zero superpowers dependency at the FLOOR; acceptance = E10.

## 2. The skill's content — the craft (where the kit's value is)

`skills/continuous-discovery/SKILL.md` encodes the partner craft and chains to the kit's own spine:

- **Discovery is the front of the loop.** Before `design` shapes a solution, ask whether this is the right *problem* and the right *outcome*. The agent is the **discovery partner**, not the decider — the human is the PO; the agent structures the space and keeps it honest.
- **★ Outcome over output (the distinctive north-star).** Frame work by the customer/business **outcome** it changes, not the feature shipped. A slice with no outcome hypothesis is output theatre — surface that. (Ties to the Definition-of-Ready "success metric / hypothesis" item.)
- **★ Opportunity solution tree (Torres).** Map outcome → `opportunity`s (problems/needs/desires) → solution ideas → experiments. The partner builds the tree with the human so the solution space is explored, not assumed; the chosen opportunity is explicit, not the first idea.
- **★ Surface the riskiest assumption.** Every plan bets on assumptions (desirability, viability, feasibility, usability). The partner names them and ranks the **riskiest assumption** — the one that, if wrong, wastes the most work — so it is tested first.
- **★ Smallest test / small bet.** Design the cheapest experiment that would prove or kill the riskiest assumption *before* building the slice (`small bet`); continuous, not big-bang — small tests on a cadence beat one upfront discovery phase.
- **Hands off to `design`, not implementation.** Discovery's terminal state is a validated problem + outcome + the riskiest assumption tested → handed to `skills/design/SKILL.md`. It never starts a solution; that is `design`'s job.
- **Chains to the kit's own artifacts:** capture intent with `templates/FEATURE-REQUEST-TEMPLATE.md`; the Definition-of-Ready "success metric / hypothesis" is the outcome bar; `conformance/discovery-complete.sh` is the gate this craft feeds; prove a claim with `skills/verification/SKILL.md`.

This is a kit-original craft that makes continuous discovery *usable as a discipline* by the human↔AI pair — the problem-space completion of the loop's front.
